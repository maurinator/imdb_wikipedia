The Third Generation (1979)

Summaries

A mogul merrily funds terrorists to boost his computer sales, by panicking West German government and industry c. 1980, as the third generation of Western European left-wing activists forms, after crippling of the violent Red Army Faction. Released in 1979, largely unavailable until 2004. International kapitalist PJ Lurz's secretary is a gang member, while her Polizeikommissar father-in-law/lover hired Lurz' corporate security force.

Reviews

For me, "The Third Generation" was not a good/beautiful film but an interesting experience.A piece of Proust's madeleine, way in a lost time, rediscovering of images, a catharsis form.The story of these wayward terrorists is the Edmondo de Amicis Cuore's experience. Same message, same innocence and trip.The story is picture of a brutal time. I saw looking this film, not only for the location of action, moments of blessed father Vincent Eugene Bossilkoff, a Bulgarian member of Congregation of the Passion, martyred on November 11, 1952 by Communist regime. I saw the shadow of a hated era and forms of resistance against the evil.Maybe, this film is a masterpiece. Or a boring show about strange gestures and facts. For "free world", the crimes of East Europe are, in fact, only form of fiction. In essence, in the deep of souls of many people, the Communism is not ghost, traumatic event or sadistic game. Like butchers or victims, it is a eternal illness. Dust in blood's composition, memories of weak power and fear.So, Hans Vielhaber's character is not a boy with a great mission but the face of purity in an ugly time.
