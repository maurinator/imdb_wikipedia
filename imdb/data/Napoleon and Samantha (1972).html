Napoleon and Samantha (1972)

Summaries

11-year old Napoleon lives with his grandfather. He has a good friend who is a clown at a circus. When the clown returns to Europe, Napoleon takes care of lion Major. But the grandfather dies and Napoleon runs off with the lion and his friend Samantha.

Napoleon lives with his grandfather and his best friend is Samantha. When they meet an aging clown who has a pet lion named Major who wants to return to Europe, they take Major. When his grandfather senses that he is dying, he tells Napoleon. After he dies, Napoleon hires a young man named Danny to help him bury his grandfather. Before leaving Danny asks Napoleon what's he going to do, he said that before dying his grandfather sent a letter to his uncle asking to take care of Napoleon. Danny tells them that he stays in the mountains in a cabin, and if they need to go see him. When the letter his grandfather sent was sent back, Napoleon decides to go see Danny but because of Major can't take the bus decides to go through the mountains. Samantha decides to join him as well.

Two young children, who, rather than part with an old pet lion who was once a circus performer, go on a perilous mountain trek to stay with a recluse friend.

Reviews

Determined boy runs away from his rural community rather than risk being placed in an orphanage, taking along his pet lion and little girlfriend for a journey through rugged terrain. Family film from Disney with nature-adventure asides never really gets going--mostly due to that sleepy lion, ambling through the picture as if drugged. The kids (talented Johnny Whitaker from "Family Affair" and wise-talking Jodie Foster in her first film role) are both good, even though they occasionally struggle with their delivery. Michael Douglas has fine, if colorless, supporting role as a well-meaning hippie-type who comes to their rescue. Younger children might enjoy it. ** from ****
