Bedknobs and Broomsticks (1971)

Summaries

During WWII in England, Charlie, Carrie, and Paul Rawlins are sent to live with Eglantine Price, an apprentice witch. Charlie blackmails Miss Price that if he is to keep her practices a secret, she must give him something, so she takes a bedknob from her late father's bed and places the "famous magic traveling spell" on it, and only Paul can activate it. Their first journey is to a street in London where they meet Emelius Browne, headmaster of Miss Price's witchcraft training correspondence school. Miss Price tells him of a plan to find the magic words for a spell known as Substitutiary Locomotion, which brings inanimate objects to life. This spell will be her work for the war effort.

London rascals Charlie Paul and Carrie are among a flood of kids evacuated to country villages, in their case Pepperinge Eye, where a lack of host families means they're assigned 'for now' to loner Eglantine Price. They discover she's an apprentice with by correspondence and promise to keep her secret only if allowed to join the fun, finding her not very gifted. Little Paul already mastered making the bed fly them anywhere by means of magical knob when the magic school is closed. Unwilling to miss out of the last chapter, "substituary locomotion", they track down founding professor Emelius to London's Portobello Street. He's a crook who never believed his spells would work, but eagerly joins the band in the hunt for the other half of the magic book he copied from but 'modernized'. It means flying to a magical animals Island, in search of the powerful start of sorcerer Astoroth. It's ti be put to use in the war effort, just in time as Germans land in the coastal town for a terror surprise mini-invasion, only to be fought off by a magical army.

In August of 1940 in the English village of Pepperinge Eye, three cockney orphans are sent to live with Eglantine Price, who is studying to become an apprentice witch. When she receives a letter from the Correspondence College of Witchcraft in London, she and the children fly on a bed (by way of a magic bedknob) to London to meet the headmaster of the defunct school, Emelius Brown. At a townhouse where Mr. Brown is staying, Miss Price finds half of a book called THE SPELLS OF ASTOROTH. For the other half, they deal with a shady character known as the Bookman.

An apprentice witch, three kids and a cynical conman search for the missing component to a magic spell useful to the defense of Britain.

Reviews

This is one of the few films where I consider the film rendition to be an improvement on the original book. The story is clear, accessible, amusing and interesting and the musical numbers are without a doubt exceptional. I adored the cyclical rendition of 'The old home guard' and the charming 'Portobello Road', a great combination of early animation + real actors techniques which, though dated do not detract from the charm of the piece. The background of the Second World War worked well and was not omitted as the film got under way, which so often happens in 'evacuee' stories.An often far too underrated film, it produces no end to enjoyment for people of all ages. The performances from the actors are exceptionally well done and the entire text is neatly tied together and well designed. Guaranteed to put a smile on your face!
