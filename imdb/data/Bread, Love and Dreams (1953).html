Bread, Love and Dreams (1953)

Summaries

Marshal Antonio Carotenuto is sent to Sagliena, an imaginary little village in the center of Italy in the '50s, to assume his new mandate. In the village he knows Maria (Frisky) AKA 'la Bersagliera', secretly in love with a policeman (Carabiniere) reporting to Antonio. But also Paoletta, the priest's nephew, loves him. Things become more complicated when Annarella, the midwife, starts demonstrating her love to Antonio. She is hiding a secret and the Marshal soon will be in a restless situation.

When a veteran marshal is sent to a small town, he quickly falls for two women: a midwife and an earthy young woman nicknamed "Frisky".

Reviews

His love affair of a youth (Gina Lollobrigida) that in turn is become infatuated with a Carabinieri youth so shy that depose his love, forms a triangle passing the across of the whole film. About these characters forms a stage that represents magnificently a rural environment of the years 50. It has a very proper cross for a rural farce that it develops in a few rural perfect stages. Then is a film that leaves a very pleasant memory.The Gina Lollobrigida actress stands out in her rule of lively and vital girl. Competes and surpasses to the role of the commander ( Vittorio de Sica ).
