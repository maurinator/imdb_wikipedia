The Hearse (1980)

Summaries

Jane Hardy decides to stay the summer in the house her aunt left her when she died, to try and recoup from a bad divorce. Little does she know, her aunt practiced witchcraft and is still thought of very badly by the town's citizens. As soon as she moves in, she is haunted by a old black hearse and it's creepy driver. Is she going insane or is she truly being menaced? She meets a friendly young man and becomes involved with him, but is he and the creepy driver one and the same?

A girl is haunted by a phantom hearse, which she believes is connected to her departed aunt, a witch with a bad reputation in town.

Reviews

Some childhood memories may best be left in your childhood. I watched this movie several years ago and remember the creepy hearse. Sadly, it was the only thing going for this movie. The concept of the movie is very creepy and builds suspense with the mysterious hearse. I like a slow build which this movie had. The only problem about a slow build is that it needs to build to a solid ending. Sadly, the ending was pathetic is this movie.Some of the build didn't work very well either due to poor acting. I wasn't too enthralled with Trish Van Devere as the lead actress or David Gautreaux as her love interest. The best character in the movie was the creepy hearse driver. Of course, they couldn't give him much screen time or the movie would lose its suspense.There are much better movies in the ghost / haunted house subgenre. I would only watch this one after exhausting the higher quality ones.
